Thursday 12th June 2025


Afterfeast of Pentecost


Liturgy of St John Chrysostom

Today we commemorate:
St. Onuphrius the Great (4th C). St. Peter of Mt. Athos (734). St. Arsenius, abbot of Konevits (1447). Holy Martyr Antonina of Nicea (305). St. John the Soldier of Egypt (7th C). Russian New Martyr Onuphrius, bishop (1938) and his co-sufferers Hieromartyrs Anthony, Barsanuphius and Joseph (1937), and Hierarch Alexander of Kharkov.
British Isles and Ireland:
St. Ternan, missionary abbot & bishop of Culross (5th C).

Today's readings:
Romans 1:28-2:9;
Matthew 5:27-32

Romans 1:28-2:9
Since those suppressing the truth … do not take God into their reckoning, God gave them up to a degenerate mind, to do what is not appropriate. They have become gorged with everything that is unrighteous: sexual immorality, malice, greed, evil; they are full of envy, murder, quarrelling, guile, malevolence; they are slanderers, backbiters, God-haters, insolent, arrogant, boastful, mischief-makers, disobedient to parents, without understanding, treacherous, hard-hearted, implacable, merciless. Knowing God’s just judgement, that those who do such things deserve death, they not only do them, but applaud others who are doing them. It follows that you yourself are without excuse, every one of you who sits in judgement. For when you judge another, you condemn yourself because you who are passing judgement are doing these same things. Now we know that God’s judgement against those who do such things accords with the truth. Do you think that you who judge those who do these things while doing them yourself will escape the condemnation of God? Or do you despise the riches of his kindness and tolerance and patience, not realising that the kindness of God is to lead you to repentance? Down in your callous and unrepentant heart you are storing up wrath for yourself on the Day of Wrath, and of revelation and the righteous judgement of God, ‘who will reward each one according to his or her deeds.’ There will be everlasting life for those who, by patient attention to good works, seek glory and honour and immortality, but acrimony and wrath for the contentious who reject the truth and embrace unrighteousness; tribulation and distress upon every human soul dedicated to evil, the Jew first and then the Greek.


Matthew 5:27-32
The Lord said, ‘You have heard that it was said to those of old, “You shall not commit adultery,” but I am telling you that anyone looking at a woman, desiring her, has already committed adultery with her in his heart. If then your right eye causes you to stumble, pluck it out and throw it away from you, for it is better for you to lose one of your members than for your whole body to be thrown into hell. And if your right hand causes you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away from you, for it is better for you to lose one of your members than for your whole body to be thrown into hell. It was also said, “Whoever divorces his wife is to give her a certificate of divorce,” but I am telling you that whoever divorces his wife, except in a case of adultery, forces her to commit adultery. And whoever marries a divorced woman commits adultery.’
